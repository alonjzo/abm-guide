02
Account Lists & the Dual-Maintenance Model

This is the single most operationally nuanced part of working in Demandbase One at TP. Understanding it upfront saves hours of frustration. The short version: advertising campaigns can only pull account lists from the Global workspace. The US Workspace is where you do your strategic work. For a list to work in a campaign, it needs to exist in both places.

The Dual-Maintenance SOP — How to Create a List That Actually Works in Campaigns
Follow these steps every time you create a new account list or person list. Skip any step and the campaign won't be able to pull the audience.
1
Confirm you're in the US Workspace
Top-left dropdown — should read "US Workspace." If it reads "Global" or anything else, switch before building. Everything you create in the wrong workspace lives there permanently.
2
Build the list in US Workspace first
Create your account list or person list with all filter logic, segments, and criteria in the US Workspace. Name it clearly — use your vertical or campaign name as a prefix so it's identifiable.
3
Duplicate the list into the Global workspace
Use the duplicate function. Switch to the Global workspace and paste or recreate the exact same list there. This is the manual step Demandbase has not automated — there is no sync between workspaces. You are doing this by hand every time.
4
Name the Global copy with the [US] prefix
The naming convention for all US assets in the Global workspace is [US] followed by the list name. Example: if the US Workspace list is "T1-A Financial Services," the Global copy becomes "[US] T1-A Financial Services." This is how the team distinguishes US assets from EMEA, APAC, and LATAM assets in the shared Global workspace.
5
Verify selectors carried over correctly
After duplicating, open the Global copy and confirm all filter logic is intact. Demandbase may warn that selector references might not work correctly after duplication — this is normal. Open each list, check the selectors, and manually correct anything that didn't transfer. SFDC-based fields carry over cleanly. Calculated Fields and Demandbase-native segments may need to be rebuilt in Global.
6
Now build your campaign — it will find the list
When setting up an advertising campaign, the audience picker pulls from Global. Your [US]-prefixed list will now appear. If it doesn't appear in the campaign builder, the Global copy either doesn't exist or the name doesn't match exactly — go back and check Step 4.
Known platform limitation: There is no automatic sync between US Workspace and Global. Any update to a list — adding accounts, changing filter criteria, refreshing segment logic — must be manually applied to both copies. This has been flagged to the Demandbase TAM team. Until it's resolved, dual-maintenance is the operational reality. Build the habit of updating both every time.
Person Lists follow the same rule. If you're building a person list for persona-based targeting (CCO outreach, CFO personas, etc.) and it needs to serve a campaign, it must exist in Global with the [US] prefix. Same six steps, same manual duplication, same verification check on selectors.

Campaign Management — How Campaigns Work at TP

Campaigns in Demandbase One for TP are account-based — meaning every campaign is targeted at a defined account list plus a persona layer, not at individual leads. Understanding this structure is what separates an ABM campaign from a generic digital campaign.

Account List + Persona = Audience
Every campaign targets the intersection of an account list and a persona. A persona applied to a T1-A account list only surfaces contacts at those T1-A accounts who match the persona's job title criteria. Broad enough persona + right account list = the right people see the ad.
Watch for: Persona criteria that are too narrow will return zero matched contacts even on a large account list. Check buying group completeness before launching.
🔑
Intent Keywords Drive Targeting
The 42+ keyword sets built in the US Workspace (and duplicated in Global) determine which accounts are surfacing in-market intent. When building a campaign, align the target account list to the keyword sets relevant to that campaign's message — don't mix intent signals with mismatched creative.
Rule: Buyer research language works. Procurement/vendor language does not — intent engines index published content, not RFP-speak.
🗺️
Journey Stage Gates Campaigns
The US Journey has seven stages. Campaign strategy should shift by stage — awareness-stage accounts get thought leadership and problem framing, mid-stage accounts get solution content, late-stage accounts get proof points and case data. Don't run the same creative across all stages.
Practical move: Use journey stage as a filter when selecting your account list. Target accounts in stages 2–4 for pipeline acceleration campaigns.
⚙️
Buying Groups Confirm Coverage
Three buying groups are live: CX Core, Lines of Business, and Vertical Specialists. Before launching a campaign, check buying group completeness on your target account list. Low completeness means the buying committee isn't covered — the campaign may reach the wrong people or miss the decision-makers entirely.
Target: Accounts with 3+ active buying group personas are your strongest MQA candidates post-campaign.

04
Intent & Engagement Reporting — What to Watch and When

Demandbase surfaces two types of signal: intent (what accounts are researching across the web) and engagement (how accounts are interacting with TP specifically). Both matter and they tell different stories. Knowing which signal to act on — and when — is the core skill of the ABM operator role.

SignalWhere to Find ItWhat It Tells YouWhat to Do With It
Intent Spike Intent module → filter by account tier or list An account is actively researching a topic relevant to TP's services — right now, before any conversation has started Flag to the relevant SDR if it's an open account, or to the BD if it's a named account. Consider accelerating ad spend against this account for the next 2 weeks.
Engagement Minutes Rising Dashboard → sort by Engagement Minutes, last 14 days Contacts at the account are accumulating interactions with TP — ads, emails, site visits, content downloads Add to your weekly MQA review list. If Engagement Minutes have been climbing for 3+ weeks, this account is warming up independently. Pull the buying group coverage report.
Journey Stage Advancement Journey module → filter by "stage changed this week" The account's collective behavior has crossed a threshold — they moved forward in the buying cycle Surface to SDR immediately. A stage advancement this week is a timing signal — reach out now, not in two weeks.
Buying Group Completeness Drop Buying Groups → sort by completeness A persona that was engaged has gone quiet — or was never covered in the first place Check if the contact left the company. If coverage is low across an account you're targeting, adjust persona criteria or add a broader job title filter to the targeting layer.
High Intent + Low Engagement Cross-reference Intent module with Dashboard engagement data An account is researching your space but isn't interacting with TP content — they may not know TP is an option Increase ad frequency against this account. This is the gap your campaigns are designed to close. Check that the account is on the right campaign audience list.
Campaign Engagement Post-Launch Advertising → campaign performance (note: clicks report blocked until One Tag is live) Which accounts are engaging with specific ad creative and messaging Use impression data and engagement metrics to identify which accounts are responding. Build your MQA list from campaign-engaged accounts 2 weeks post-launch.
Weekly rhythm to build: Check the Dashboard every Monday for engagement trends. Check Intent every Wednesday for new spikes. Pull journey stage changes every Friday and share with the SDR team lead. This 3-touch weekly cadence keeps the MQA pipeline from going stale between campaign runs.
05
MQA Coordination — How Marketing Hands Off to Sales

The MQA (Marketing Qualified Account) is the handoff point between the ABM program and Sales. Marketing brings the account to the door — SDRs and BD take it from there. Your job is to make sure the signal is clean and the context travels with it.

The 2-Week Window
After a campaign activates, wait 2 weeks before pulling the MQA list. This gives the campaign time to generate engagement signal. SDRs review the list at the 2-week mark — they need that context baked in before they reach out.
Your action: Mark campaign launch dates and set a calendar reminder for the 2-week MQA review. Share the filtered account list with the SDR lead on that date.
📋
What to Include in the Handoff
Don't just hand SDRs a list of account names. Include: what the account engaged with, which personas showed activity, what intent keywords they were researching, and the account's current journey stage. Context converts — a cold list does not.
Format: Export from Demandbase as a filtered account view with engagement and intent columns visible. Or share a saved dashboard view the SDR can access directly.
🔒
Named Account Rule for SDRs
SDRs can work any open account freely. If an MQA is a named BD account (visible in SFDC), the SDR coordinates with the BD before engaging. Your role: flag named account MQAs separately from open account MQAs when you share the list. Don't mix them.
Practice: Tag named BD accounts in your MQA export so the SDR lead doesn't have to look them up individually in SFDC.
📡
Ongoing Signal Sharing
MQAs aren't a one-time handoff. Intent spikes happen between campaigns. Journey stage advancements happen while campaigns are in flight. Keep a standing Slack channel or weekly sync with the SDR lead so real-time signals don't sit in the platform without action.
Quick win: Set up Demandbase Slack alert integrations for your top T1-A and Tier 1 accounts so intent spikes surface automatically without manual checking.
The feedback loop matters: When SDRs work an MQA and learn something — wrong contact, budget shift, bad timing — that context should come back to Marketing. It tells you whether the campaign messaging landed with the right persona at the right stage. Without feedback, you're optimizing campaigns blind.
