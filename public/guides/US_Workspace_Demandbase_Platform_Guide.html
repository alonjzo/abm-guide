Section 02

Asset Connections: Global ↔ US Workspace

During the workspace setup process, Step 3 ("Connect Assets") determines which lists, filters, and reports are shared between Global and the US Workspace. If an asset isn't connected here, Global-level features like Advertising can't reference it — even though you can see the list exists.

Issue Found — March 2, 2026 Multiple US Workspace account lists were visible from Global but not connected as shared assets. This prevented the Advertising module from using US account lists as campaign targeting audiences. The fix: navigate to Workspaces → Step 3: Connect Assets and check the boxes on all relevant account lists, people lists, opportunity lists, and filters.
Resolution All priority account lists have been connected between Global and the US Workspace. When duplicating assets between workspaces, the platform may warn that selector references might not work correctly — this is normal. After duplication, open each list and verify that all selectors and field references are intact. SFDC-based field logic (industry, tier, account owner) carries over cleanly. Demandbase-native fields like Calculated Fields and Segments may need to be rebuilt in the destination workspace.
Section 03

Platform Modules — Team Reference

Dashboard

Your command center

The Dashboard shows account activity, engagement trends, and pipeline health across the US Workspace in real time. This is where you start every session.

  • Filter by dynamic account lists (T1-A, Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3) to see which accounts are heating up right now
  • Set default views by service line — CX Core, RaaS, TaaS — so each team member lands on what's relevant to them
  • Track buying group completeness at a glance to identify coverage gaps in the buying committee
  • Verticals Build saved views per vertical (Healthcare, Insurance, Banking/FS, Government, Retail, Utilities) so BDRs aren't sorting through the entire account universe to find their lane
  • Sales Play Check the dashboard before every outreach call. If a Tier 1 account spiked engagement this week, that's your opening — not a cold pitch

Engagement

How actively accounts interact with TP

Engagement measures web visits, ad clicks, email opens, content downloads, and other touchpoints. Engagement Minutes are the currency. The rules are global across workspaces, but you can isolate US-specific activity through Calculated Fields and Segments.

  • Sort accounts by engagement velocity — the rate of change, not just total minutes — to catch accounts accelerating before competitors do
  • Cross-reference engagement against tier to find underperforming Tier 1s (low engagement = awareness gap) and overperforming Tier 3s (potential upgrade candidates)
  • Verticals Compare engagement patterns across verticals — Healthcare accounts tend to engage deeply on fewer pieces; Financial Services spreads wider across topics
  • LOB Accounts engaging with CX content AND AI/automation content simultaneously signal a multi-service-line deal — route to the right team
  • Sales Play "Your account went from 12 engagement minutes to 87 in two weeks" is a real conversation opener. Time outreach to the buying window, not your calendar

Site Analytics

Who's on our website — even without a form fill

Site Analytics shows which accounts are visiting teleperformance.com, what pages they view, and how deep they go. This is the earliest signal that an account is in-market — most B2B buyers are 70%+ through their journey before they talk to sales.

  • Map page visits to service lines: accounts viewing CX content vs. AI/automation pages vs. vertical case studies tell you which motion to lead with
  • Identify anonymous Tier 1 and T1-A account traffic to see who's researching but hasn't engaged directly
  • Verticals If a Healthcare account starts reading Insurance case studies, that's a cross-sell signal. If a CX-only account visits RaaS or TaaS pages, the deal is expanding
  • Sales Play Page-level intelligence before outreach changes the game. "I noticed your team has been evaluating contact center AI solutions" beats a generic intro every time

Intent

What your accounts are researching across the open web

Third-party intent signals show what topics accounts are actively researching — powered by 40+ keyword sets with nearly 2,000 keywords organized around buyer research patterns across all TP service lines and verticals.

  • Watch for intent surges (spikes above baseline) rather than raw scores — a Tier 2 account surging on "contact center outsourcing" is more actionable than a Tier 1 with steady-state intent
  • Cross-set keyword overlap is intentional — accounts researching "AI customer service" light up both CX and TaaS keyword sets, signaling a multi-service-line opportunity
  • Keywords are organized around buyer research patterns, not internal service taxonomy — this is the "buyer brain" vs. "seller brain" distinction that drives better signal
  • Verticals Vertical keyword sets (Healthcare CX, Insurance claims automation, Banking digital transformation) surface accounts researching industry-specific solutions vs. generic CX — this determines which persona and pitch to lead with
  • Sales Play Intent is your "why now" for outreach. Sort by intent surge + tier, and shift the message from "let me tell you about TP" to "I see you're evaluating options for [specific topic] — here's how we solved that"

Reports

Proving the work is working

Campaign attribution, pipeline influence, engagement trends, and conversion metrics across the funnel. Reports live inside the US Workspace — if you're in Global, you won't see your saved US reports.

  • Build reports around the tiering structure — pipeline from T1-A vs. Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 shows whether targeting is sharp or if deals are coming from off-list accounts
  • Track campaign-to-pipeline attribution to connect ABM activation directly to revenue for leadership visibility
  • Engagement-to-opportunity conversion rates by service line reveal which motions drive real deals vs. activity theater
  • Verticals Vertical-level pipeline reports answer the "where should we invest" question — if Healthcare converts at 2x the rate of Retail at half the engagement cost, that's a resource allocation decision
  • Sales Play Share reports with sales leadership to build platform trust. When reps see that high engagement + intent surge accounts convert 3x faster, they stop ignoring the tool and start using it daily

Journeys

The account-level funnel — signal-based progression

Journey Stages track where every account sits in the buying cycle: Target Account → Aware → Engaged → MQA → Opportunity → Customer. The US Journey is a dedicated funnel for the US motion, separate from Global, EMEA, and APAC journeys.

  • Journey stages replace gut feel with signal-based progression — each stage is defined by specific engagement, intent, and pipeline criteria
  • Use journey stage changes as orchestration triggers — when an account moves from Engaged to MQA, automated SFDC tasks fire and BDRs get notified
  • Pushing additional journey funnels into Salesforce requires Account Segments → Calculated Fields → SFDC field mapping (coordinate with Salesforce admin team on field creation)
  • LOB Separate journey logic for CX motion vs. Vertical/Transformation motion — a Healthcare digital transformation deal has a different progression pattern than standard CX outsourcing
  • Sales Play Marketing owns Unengaged → Engaged. Sales lives in MQA and Opportunity. The handoff at MQA is visible and tracked — no more Slack messages that get buried
Section 04

MQA Stage — Issue & Resolution

On March 2, the US Marketing ABX Dashboard showed 1 MQA account out of 5,241 total. Accounts were jumping from Engaged (333) directly to Opportunity (237), bypassing MQA entirely. The root cause: the MQA stage qualification thresholds were set too aggressively for the current state of the US data model.

Root Cause The MQA stage used a tiered qualification model with AND-stacked conditions. Every condition within a tier path had to be true simultaneously. The combination of high engagement thresholds, HIGH-only intent requirements, and the Engaged Known People >= 2 gate created a near-impossible qualification path. Only 1 account across all tiers met every condition.

MQA Selector Logic

-- US Journey: MQA Stage Logic
-- Pattern: ( 1 AND 2 AND ( 3 OR 4 )) OR ( 5 AND 6 ) OR ( 7 AND 8 )

PATH 1: T1-A / Tier 1 Accounts
  Engagement Points >= 50 in past 3 months
  AND Intent Strength = HIGH
  AND Engaged Known People >= 2
  AND (2026 T1-A Contains US Market OR 2026 Tier 1 Contains US Market)

PATH 2: Tier 2 Accounts
  Engagement Points >= 30 in past 3 months
  AND Intent Strength = HIGH, MED
  AND 2026 Tier 2 Contains US Market

PATH 3: Tier 3 Accounts
  Engagement Points >= 15 in past 3 months
  AND Intent Strength = HIGH, MED, LOW
  AND 2026 Tier 3 Contains US Market

Recommended Threshold Adjustments

Tier Path Criteria Previous Recommended Rationale
T1-A / Tier 1 Engagement Points ≥ 50 ≥ 30 Aligns with Tier 2 threshold; lets strong accounts qualify earlier
Intent Strength HIGH only HIGH or MED HIGH intent is a spike, not sustained — MED captures active research
Engaged Known People ≥ 2 ≥ 1 Removes penalty for data coverage gaps; buying groups handle multi-threading
Tier 2 Engagement Points ≥ 30 ≥ 20 ~30% reduction to open flow; can tighten once data stabilizes
Tier 3 Engagement Points ≥ 15 ≥ 10 Low barrier appropriate for growth-tier prospecting motion
Expected Outcome After adjustment, MQA should populate with 30–80 accounts depending on current engagement and intent distribution. Monitor for 48–72 hours after publishing and tighten thresholds if the funnel floods. The goal is healthy flow, not perfection — you need data moving through the stage before you can optimize against it.
Section 05

Advertising — Global Architecture

The Demandbase Advertising module (B2B DSP) operates at the tenant/Global level, not inside individual workspaces. This means ad campaigns are built and managed from Global, and targeting audiences must either be created at the Global level or connected from workspaces via Step 3: Connect Assets.

$

Budget Governance

Ad spend flows through a single DSP contract at the tenant level. Impression delivery, frequency caps, and spend allocation are controlled globally across all regions.

Audience Architecture

The advertising module uses the same cookie/IP-to-account graph at the tenant level. Workspace-level ad accounts would risk bidding against yourself across regions.

→

Using US Lists for Ads

Connect US Workspace account lists to Global via Step 3: Connect Assets. Once connected, those lists become available as audience sources in the Advertising campaign builder.

Quick Start

For immediate campaigns: build targeting lists at Global level using the same SFDC field logic. For long-term: use Segments → Calculated Fields → SFDC field mapping as a bridge.

Open Item Workspace-level account lists are visible from Global but require asset connection to be usable for ad targeting. This is a configuration step, not a product limitation. Ensure all priority targeting lists are connected before building ad campaigns.
