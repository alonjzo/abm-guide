01
Your Account Universe — What You Own in the Platform

The US Workspace runs approximately 5,200 tiered accounts. Your world is the top of that universe — the most strategic, highest-revenue accounts in the program. These are not marketing targets. These are relationships in motion, and the platform gives you live visibility into what's happening at each one.

BD Primary Territory
T1-A Accounts
Revenue: $50B and above
The enterprise crown jewels. These accounts get the highest ad frequency, the most intent monitoring, and the most deliberate campaign sequencing. If they're named to you, SDRs know to coordinate with you before making contact.
BD Primary Territory
Tier 1 Accounts
Revenue: $10B – $49.9B
Large enterprise accounts with complex buying committees and longer cycles. Demandbase gives you multi-persona visibility — you can see which part of the buying group is most active right now. That's where a conversation is most likely to land.

Named Accounts — SDRs Know to Check In First

SDRs have full access to research and work accounts across the platform. Where it applies to you: if an account is formally assigned to you in SFDC, SDRs know to reach out before engaging. That's not a policy they need to be reminded of — it's how the team operates. When an SDR brings you an MQA from one of your named accounts, they're handing you a qualified signal. Here's what to evaluate:

  • Which campaign drove the engagement — was it the right messaging for this account?
  • Which personas are showing up as active — does that match who you're already in conversations with?
  • Is the intent topic aligned to an active conversation — or is this a different part of the business entirely?
  • Is the timing right — or would SDR outreach now disrupt an existing thread?
02
What the Platform Shows You — The BD Intelligence Layer

Every view in Demandbase is telling you something about account health and buying behavior. Here's how each module translates to something actionable for your BD work.

📡
Intent Intelligence
What accounts are researching right now
Demandbase monitors keyword activity across the web — not just on TP's site. When a T1-A account spikes on "AI-powered customer experience," "contact center transformation," or "outsourcing strategy," that surge shows up in real time, weeks before they'd typically signal buying intent through traditional channels.
BD use: An intent spike is often the early signal of an internal initiative forming. If your contact hasn't mentioned it yet, they may not be the champion — or the initiative may be running above them.
🧩
Buying Group Coverage
Who inside the account is active
Buying Groups in Demandbase track three persona clusters — CX Core (CCOs, VP CX), Lines of Business, and Vertical Specialists. When multiple personas inside one account show engagement, it signals that a buying conversation is spreading through the organization. One engaged persona is curiosity. Three is a committee.
BD use: If the CFO persona at one of your T1-A accounts starts engaging, that's a different conversation than CX engagement. It may mean a deal you thought was CX-driven is actually a finance-led cost reduction play.
Engagement Minutes
Total account warmth over time
Engagement Minutes aggregate every interaction an account has had with TP — ads, emails, site visits, content. It's a cumulative warmth score. An account that's been quietly building Engagement Minutes over 6 weeks without a formal conversation may be further along the evaluation track than you think.
BD use: Before a QBR or executive meeting, check your account's Engagement Minutes trend. If it's climbing without a corresponding sales conversation, someone in that account is doing due diligence on their own.
🗺️
Journey Stage
Where the account sits in the buying cycle
The US Workspace runs a seven-stage account journey. A stage advancement means the account's collective behavior has moved them forward in the buying cycle — more engagement, more intent, more committee activity. Stage changes are tracked so you can see when momentum shifts.
BD use: A Tier 1 account that advances two stages in three weeks is in motion. That's the window to accelerate your engagement, not wait for their next check-in request.
AI Account Insights
Automated account intelligence brief
Demandbase's AI panel inside each account profile synthesizes intent trends, recent engagement, buying group activity, and journey stage into a plain-language summary. It's designed to surface patterns that would take 20 minutes to manually piece together — in about 30 seconds.
BD use: Pull this before any executive call. If you've been in a relationship for two years, the AI brief may surface something that's shifted in the last 30 days that you haven't heard directly yet.
Pipeline Predict Score
Predictive model for account readiness
Pipeline Predict uses historical engagement patterns, firmographic data, and intent signals to score how likely an account is to enter pipeline in the next 90 days. Two models run independently in the US Workspace — one for the CX motion, one for Vertical/Transformation.
BD use: Use the Pipeline Predict score to prioritize which accounts warrant an accelerated outreach sequence vs. which ones are in a slower-moving watch pattern.

04
The Marketing Partnership — How This Actually Works

Marketing is running coordinated campaigns against your accounts right now. Targeted ads, personalized content, intent-triggered sequences — all built to create the context that makes your conversations land. This isn't Marketing working in parallel to Sales. It's Marketing setting the table before you sit down.

Marketing's Role
Warming the Account Before You Arrive
By the time a T1-A account surfaces as an MQA, they've already seen TP messaging multiple times — ads targeted to their industry, content mapped to their intent signals, engagement tracked back to specific personas. Your first meaningful conversation is not the first time they've encountered Teleperformance.
BD's Role
Converting Relationship into Pipeline
You bring something Demandbase can't automate — the trusted relationship, the executive credibility, the institutional knowledge of what that account actually needs. Marketing gets them engaged. You're the one who gets them to commit. The data just tells you when the window is open.
SDR's Role
The Qualification Engine
SDRs work the full open account universe and review engaged accounts 2 weeks post-campaign to qualify MQAs. For your named accounts, they'll bring those signals to you before making contact. They're your early-warning system on what's heating up — and on open accounts, they're moving pipeline forward independently.
Shared Platform
Same Data, Same Accounts, One Truth
You, Marketing, and SDRs are all looking at the same account intelligence. When an account you're working spikes on intent, Marketing sees it and may increase ad pressure. When you close a deal, that account's journey stage updates for everyone. No information silos. No competing versions of account status.
Campaign Intelligence Access: After each campaign activation, you can see which of your accounts engaged, what content resonated, and how the engagement maps to the personas inside the buying committee. This is the briefing you should be pulling before every executive conversation — not after.
