Getting Started with Advanced Reporting

Advanced Reporting is a Looker-embedded analytics layer sitting inside the Demandbase Advertising module. Unlike the standard workspace reports — which you build manually — these are pre-structured BI dashboards that read directly from your DSP data. Four views, each with a specific job.

Where to Find It

Navigate to Advertising → Advanced Reporting in the left sidebar. You must be in the Global workspace (not US Workspace) to access the Advertising module. Advanced Reporting will show a "Beta" tag — that's expected.

  4. 4
    Select Your Dashboard and Click "Get Started"

    Four tile options appear: Country-Level Reporting, Creative Performance, JourneyIQ Reporting, and Pacing & Account Performance. Click the tile you need, then hit the "Get Started" button to open the Looker view.

  5. 5
    Set Your Date Range and Filters

    Every dashboard has a date range picker in the top-right. Default is last 30 days. US team: always filter to the TP USA ad plan or US account list before reading any numbers — otherwise you're seeing global spend data.

US Attribution Gap — Read This First

The Demandbase One Tag (GTM-5J4WZ38R) has not yet been deployed on tp.com/en-us as of March 2026. This means advertising clicks and site-attribution data will show zero in these dashboards for US campaigns. Impression delivery and spend data will still populate. Once the One Tag is live, the click and attribution columns will hydrate retroactively from that date forward.

SECTION 02

Country-Level Reporting

This dashboard slices your advertising performance by country or region. For a global organization running campaigns across multiple geos, it answers the most basic and most important question: where is my money actually going, and is it landing where my target accounts are?

What You'll See

  • KPI Scorecard Row — Total impressions, clicks, CTR, spend, and CPM aggregated across your selected date range, filterable by campaign or ad plan
  • Geographic Heat Map — World map with color intensity tied to impression volume by country. Darker = more delivery
  • Top 10 Countries Bar Chart — Ranked by impressions or spend (togglable). Quick read on where your DSP is allocating budget
  • Country-Level Data Table — Full breakdown: country, impressions, clicks, CTR, spend, CPM — sortable by any column
Metric What It Tells You US Team Action
Impressions by Country Where the DSP is delivering your ads across the IP/cookie graph Verify United States is dominant share — if EMEA countries show high volume, check campaign geo targeting settings
CTR by Country Which geographies are generating click engagement vs. passive views Low US CTR is expected pre-One Tag — use this as your baseline; watch it move after GTM deploys
Spend by Country Budget allocation across geographies by the DSP bidding algorithm Should mirror your account list geography — if spend is skewing outside US, flag to TAM
CPM by Country Cost per 1,000 impressions — efficiency of delivery per geo US enterprise CPM is typically $8–15 for B2B programmatic. Above $20 warrants a targeting review
Best Practice

Filter this view to your specific ad plan (TP USA – Q-81341) before reading any numbers. Without filtering, you're looking at global tenant spend — CEMEA's $98K in delivery will dominate every chart and make your US data invisible.

SECTION 03

Creative Performance

This is where you find out whether your creative is actually working. Performance metrics broken down at the ad group and individual creative level — so you can identify the high-impact assets and cut what's underperforming before it drains budget.

What Populates Now

Ad group KPIs, impression counts, frequency data, and spend per creative unit will populate as long as campaigns are running. Click data will be zero until the One Tag deploys.

What's Still Empty

CTR, click-through volume, and any metric requiring site-side attribution will show zero or "—" until GTM fires the Demandbase One Tag on tp.com/en-us.

Four Views Inside This Dashboard

  • Ad Group KPIs — Impressions, spend, frequency by ad group
  • Top 5 by Impressions vs CTR — Scatter or ranked view showing which creatives have volume AND engagement
  • Trending Line Chart — Performance over time per creative unit — spots fatigue before it tanks delivery
  • Weekly Report Table — Weekly cadence breakdown for all active creatives with delta vs. prior week

How to Read Creative Performance

Signal What It Means Action
High Impressions, High CTR Your top performer — the DSP is delivering it and accounts are engaging Increase budget weight toward this creative. Use as template for next iteration
High Impressions, Low CTR Good reach, poor resonance — message isn't connecting or audience is wrong Test a new headline or offer. Consider swapping the creative to a different audience segment
Low Impressions, High CTR High quality, low volume — DSP isn't allocating enough to it Manually increase impression weight or move to a higher-priority ad group
Declining Trend Line Creative fatigue — the same accounts have seen it too many times Check frequency cap settings. Rotate in a fresh creative variant
High Frequency, Low CTR Overexposure — accounts are ignoring it Cap frequency at 3–5 impressions per account per week for enterprise B2B
US Creative Library Note

Creative assets for US campaigns live in the Advertising → Creative Library. Before running Creative Performance analysis, confirm all US creatives are named with the [US] prefix convention — otherwise this dashboard will surface EMEA and APAC creatives alongside yours and the comparison becomes meaningless.

Three Views Inside JourneyIQ Reporting

  • Funnel Visualization — Account volume at each stage with campaign influence attribution layered in. Shows whether ads are touching accounts that later progress — your "did advertising help move this?" answer
  • Ad Group by Journey Stage — Which ad groups are influencing accounts at which funnel stage. A Tier 1 account at MQA seeing your CX Core ad group is a different signal than a Tier 3 at Aware — this view surfaces that
  • Account-Level Journey Data — Drill into individual accounts to see their stage, which campaigns touched them, and when. This is the data your BD team needs before outreach

JourneyIQ vs. Standard Journey Report

The standard Journey report inside your US Workspace shows account stage distribution across all accounts. JourneyIQ Reporting in Advanced Reporting specifically overlays campaign influence — it answers "which accounts that advertising touched also progressed through the funnel?" That's a materially different and more powerful question.

SECTION 05

Pacing & Account Performance

Your campaign health dashboard. Shows whether active campaigns are on pace to hit their impression or spend goals by end of the flight — and which accounts within those campaigns are receiving delivery and generating engagement.

Why This Matters Right Now

The US advertising plan Q-81341 has $50,000 allocated across 21 campaigns with $13,240.88 in actual spend. That's 26% burn against a fully committed budget. Pacing & Account Performance is where you verify whether the remaining campaigns are delivering on schedule or sitting allocated but underperforming.

Pacing Status Definitions

Status What It Means Action Required
On Track Campaign is delivering impressions at a rate consistent with hitting the goal by flight end Monitor. No immediate action unless targeting or creative changes are planned
Under-Pacing Delivery velocity is too low — at current pace, the campaign will not spend its full budget or hit its impression goal Check targeting breadth, bid floors, creative approval status, and audience size. Bring to TAM if persistent
Predicted to Hit Goal Demandbase's DSP forecasting model predicts the campaign will reach its goal based on current delivery trend — even if current spend looks low Trust the model but verify — check if delivery is front- or back-loaded in the flight window

Account-Level Drill-Down

Below the campaign pacing summary, a table breaks out individual account performance within your campaigns. For ABM programs, this is the view that matters most — it shows you which named accounts are actually receiving impressions and generating engagement activity.

Column What to Look For
Account Name Cross-reference against your T1-A and Tier 1 lists — if these accounts are not receiving delivery, your targeting may be too narrow or bid floors too low
Impressions Minimum threshold for T1-A accounts should be 500+ impressions per month to maintain brand presence with key buying groups
Engagement Score Account-level engagement reflecting ad interactions. Rising scores in MQA-stage accounts should trigger immediate BDR notification
Spend Against Account Allocations skewing heavily toward a few accounts while others receive nothing signals audience list imbalance or bid competition from other regions
Pro Move

Export the account-level table from Pacing & Account Performance and cross-reference against your Buying Group Insights heatmap in the US Workspace. Accounts receiving high impression volume but showing zero persona engagement in Buying Group Insights are being served ads to the wrong contacts — your persona targeting needs adjustment.

SECTION 06

Using Filters Effectively

Every dashboard in Advanced Reporting shares the same filter bar at the top. Without filtering, you're looking at full tenant data — all regions, all campaigns, all time. For US team members, filters are not optional. They're how you isolate your signal from the noise.

Available Filter Dimensions

  • Ad Plan — Filter to TP USA – Q-81341 for all US campaign analysis. This is your primary filter for every session
  • Campaign — Drill into specific campaign names within the US ad plan
  • Date Range — Last 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or custom range. Always set this before reading any trend data
  • Country / Region — In Country-Level Reporting, filter to United States to isolate US delivery metrics from global totals
  • Ad Group — Filter to CX Core, LOBs, or Vertical Specialists ad groups to see buying-group-specific creative performance
  • Creative / Asset — In Creative Performance, filter to a specific banner or ad unit to isolate its delivery and engagement history

Common Filter Mistake

Selecting a date range without setting an Ad Plan filter first. The date range will apply globally and your US metrics will be diluted by CEMEA's $98K+ delivery history. Always set Ad Plan → TP USA before anything else.

📌
Bookmark Your Filtered Views

Looker allows you to save filtered dashboard states as bookmarks. Set your standard US filters once, save the view, and share the link with your team so everyone starts from the same scoped data — not the raw global view.

🔄
Data Refreshes ~Every 24 Hours

Looker data in Advanced Reporting is not real-time. It reflects DSP data from approximately the prior day. Don't use this for intraday campaign decisions — use the standard Campaigns view for live delivery checks.

Cross-Reference with Buying Group Insights

Advanced Reporting shows advertising metrics. Buying Group Insights in your US Workspace shows people engagement. Running both views in parallel tells a complete story: are the right accounts seeing ads AND are the right contacts engaging?

Export for Offline Analysis

Every Looker table has a three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner that allows CSV or Excel export. For account-level pacing data, export and cross-reference against your SFDC account owner assignments before sending to BDR teams.

JourneyIQ Is Your Leslie Metric

When reporting up, lead with JourneyIQ. Impressions and CTR are operational metrics. "Advertising influenced 14 accounts that progressed to MQA this quarter" is the business outcome metric that resonates with executive audiences.

🔍
Frequency Caps Protect Your Data Quality

High frequency with low CTR in Creative Performance is often a sign that frequency caps aren't set correctly. Enterprise B2B best practice: 3–5 impressions per account per week. Anything above that inflates impression numbers without improving engagement.

SECTION 08

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions that will come up on every team call, global sync, and new-user onboarding session — answered straight.

How often does the data in Advanced Reporting refresh?
Looker-powered dashboards in Advanced Reporting refresh approximately every 24 hours, pulling from the Demandbase DSP data pipeline. There is no real-time data in these views. For live campaign delivery checks, use the standard Campaigns view under Advertising. If data appears stale beyond 48 hours, submit a support ticket — it may indicate a pipeline processing issue on Demandbase's end.
Why is my click data showing zero across all dashboards?
For the US instance specifically, click data is zero because the Demandbase One Tag (GTM-5J4WZ38R) has not yet been deployed on tp.com/en-us as of March 2026. The tag is managed through Google Tag Manager and requires deployment by the team controlling GTM access. This is a known open item. Impression and spend data will still populate correctly — only site-side attribution (clicks, engagement, page-level tracking) is affected. Once the One Tag fires, click data will begin populating from that date forward.
Can I export data from these dashboards?
Yes. Each Looker table has a three-dot menu (⋮) or download icon in its top-right corner that allows export to CSV, Excel, or PDF. Note that downloaded data reflects the current filter state — always verify your filters are set correctly before exporting. Exports do not include visualization/chart images, only raw data tables.
What does "All Other" mean in the JourneyIQ funnel?
"All Other" in the JourneyIQ funnel represents accounts that are in your targeting universe but have not yet been classified into a named journey stage. This is normal — it's your unactivated pool. A large "All Other" bucket at the top of the funnel is not a problem; it represents accounts that your advertising will touch over time and begin moving into Aware and Engaged stages. Monitor this number decreasing over time as your campaigns mature.
I see APAC and EMEA buying group tabs in my view — is something wrong?
Nothing is broken. Buying groups are configured at the tenant level in Demandbase One, which means all regions' buying group tabs appear in every workspace's Buying Group Insights view. When using Advanced Reporting in the Advertising module (which is also tenant-level), you may see cross-regional buying group labels. The fix is to always apply your Ad Plan filter (TP USA – Q-81341) and confirm your account list filter is set to a US-scoped list before reading any data. The underlying data stays scoped to your filter selection — the tab labels are a display artifact of the tenant-wide architecture.
What does "Predicted to Hit Goal" mean — and can I trust it?
"Predicted to Hit Goal" is Demandbase's DSP forecasting model estimating that a campaign will reach its impression or spend goal by the end of the flight window, based on current delivery trajectory. You can generally trust it for campaigns that have been running for 7+ days and have established a delivery pattern. For newer campaigns (under 5 days) or campaigns that recently had targeting or creative changes, treat the prediction with more skepticism — the model needs consistent data to forecast accurately. Always pair the prediction with a manual check of the spend-to-goal ratio and days remaining in the flight.
Why does my Country-Level data show spend in countries outside the US?
This can happen for two reasons. First, if you haven't filtered to the TP USA ad plan (Q-81341), you're seeing global tenant spend — which includes CEMEA, EMEA, and other regions' campaigns. Apply the Ad Plan filter first. Second, even within a US-targeted campaign, the Demandbase DSP may deliver a small percentage of impressions to international IPs that match your account list targets — this is because some US enterprises have international IP ranges for their corporate networks. If non-US delivery is above 10% of your total impressions, review your campaign geo-targeting settings and bring it to Dusty.
Can I set up automated alerts from these Looker dashboards?
Looker has native alerting functionality that allows you to set threshold-based email alerts (e.g., "notify me if a campaign drops below X impressions per day"). Whether this is enabled in Demandbase's embedded Looker instance depends on how Demandbase has configured the integration. Ask Dusty or Nick Abernathy whether scheduled reports or threshold alerts are available in the current beta configuration — this is worth enabling for under-pacing detection.
Is Advanced Reporting available to all Demandbase users or only admins?
Access to Advanced Reporting is controlled by Demandbase user role permissions. In the TP tenant, advertising module access is typically available to users with Advertising or Admin permissions. If a team member navigates to Advertising and doesn't see the Advanced Reporting option in the sub-nav, their user role likely doesn't include advertising access. Contact your Demandbase CSM (Kate Varvedo) to request a role adjustment — specifying the user's name, email, and the "Advanced Reporting / Advertising module access" they need.
