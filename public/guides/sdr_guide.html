01
What Demandbase Is — And What It Means for Your Day

Most outreach is guesswork. Demandbase eliminates that. It monitors thousands of accounts across the web, tracking which companies are actively researching topics relevant to what Teleperformance sells — before a single person on that account ever fills out a form or picks up the phone. That signal is called intent. Your job starts there.

Intent Signals
Demandbase monitors over 137,000 keywords across the web. When accounts spike on topics like "customer experience transformation," "contact center AI," or "outsourcing CX," that spike shows up in the platform as intent activity. These aren't guesses — they're behavioral signals.
Your move: Check intent scores before any outreach. A spike this week means someone is actively researching right now.
Engagement Minutes
Every time someone from a target account visits the TP website, reads a piece of content, clicks an ad, or opens an email — those interactions accumulate as Engagement Minutes. Think of it as a heat score for how warm an account actually is.
Your move: Accounts with climbing engagement in the last 2 weeks are the ones to prioritize in your outreach queue.
🏷️
MQA — Marketing Qualified Account
An MQA is an account that has crossed a threshold of intent + engagement that tells us it is ready for a Sales conversation. This is the handoff point. Marketing brings them to the door — you're the one who knocks.
Your move: MQAs are your primary qualification target. Review MQA lists 2 weeks after every campaign activation.
🧩
Journey Stages
Every account in Demandbase moves through seven stages — from first awareness all the way to pipeline. The stage tells you how far along a buying cycle that account is. You're working accounts in the early-to-mid stages. BD takes them from there.
Your move: Filter the dashboard by journey stage to see which accounts just moved forward — that's a trigger to reach out.
02
Enterprise SDR: The BD Partnership Protocol

T1-A and Tier 1 Accounts Require BD Approval Before You Work Them

These are Teleperformance's most strategic enterprise relationships. Business Development has spent years building the foundation and trust inside these accounts. Your role is to support that motion — not operate independently of it. Before engaging any T1-A or Tier 1 account, confirm with your BD partner that the account is available for SDR activity. This is not a suggestion. It protects the relationship and it protects you.

Once you have BD clearance on a T1-A or Tier 1 account, Demandbase will show you exactly what that account is engaging with and who the most active contacts are. That context is what makes the difference between a cold touch and a relevant one.

How the partnership works in practice: BD owns the account strategy and the executive relationship. You own the top-of-funnel qualification and early engagement. When an MQA rises inside a T1-A account, flag it to your BD partner first. They'll tell you whether to engage directly, hang back, or support from a different angle.

Your Workflow: The 2-Week Post-Campaign Window

Marketing runs a campaign. Accounts engage with the ads, the content, the follow-up sequences. Then two weeks pass — and that's when you go into Demandbase. Here's exactly what that sequence looks like.

1
Campaign Goes Live
Marketing activates a targeted campaign — ads, content, email sequences — against a specific account list and persona set. You don't need to do anything yet. Let the campaign run and collect signal.
Week 0 — Campaign Active
2
Log Into Demandbase — Check Engaged Accounts
Open the US Workspace dashboard. Filter account lists by the campaign's target tier. Look at Engagement Minutes over the last 14 days. Sort by accounts showing the highest spike in activity since the campaign launched. These are your warm accounts.
Week 2 — Your Window Opens
3
Review Intent + Buying Group Coverage
For each engaged account, check the Intent module to see which keywords they've been researching. Cross-reference with Buying Group data to see how many personas inside that account are showing activity. An account with 3+ active personas and rising intent is a strong MQA candidate.
Week 2 — Qualification Layer
4
Qualify the MQA
An account hits MQA status when intent + engagement cross the threshold set by Marketing. Confirm the account meets MQA criteria based on what you see in the platform. Document your reasoning — what signals tipped it over the line. This creates a clean handoff record.
Week 2–3 — MQA Confirmation
5
Hand Off to BD
Qualified MQA goes to your BD partner with context: what they engaged with, which personas were active, what intent topics they were researching, and the account's current journey stage. BD takes it from there. The cleaner your handoff, the faster the deal moves.
Week 3 — Handoff to BD

05
How Marketing and Sales Work Together in This System

This platform isn't a Sales tool that Marketing also uses. It's the shared infrastructure that connects both teams around the same account data, the same signals, and the same definition of what "ready" looks like. That alignment is what makes the whole motion work.

📡
Marketing Warms the Account
Before you ever reach out, Marketing has already been running targeted ads, content, and email sequences against these accounts. By the time an MQA surfaces, the account has seen TP messaging multiple times. You're not the first impression — you're the conversion.
Why it matters: Your outreach lands in a context the account already recognizes. Use that.
🔄
You Feed the Signal Back
When you work an MQA and learn something — the contact is no longer there, the budget shifted, the timing is wrong — that context helps Marketing refine targeting. The system improves because both sides are feeding it. A good handoff note is as valuable as the MQA itself.
Why it matters: Marketing re-targets based on what Sales learns. Bad intel wastes budget for everyone.
🤝
Shared Account View
You and Marketing are looking at the same accounts in the same platform. If an account you've been working spikes on intent this week, Marketing sees it and may accelerate ad spend against them. If Marketing pulls back on an account, there's usually a reason — check before doubling down.
Why it matters: Coordination prevents both teams from pulling in opposite directions on the same account.
Same Definition of Pipeline
In most companies, Marketing counts leads and Sales counts deals — and they argue about the gap in between. In this model, both teams measure account engagement, MQA conversion, and pipeline influence from the same data set. One truth. No debate.
Why it matters: You can show your BD partner exactly what the account's engagement history looked like before you handed it off.
