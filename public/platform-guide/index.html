01 · Architecture

How the workspace is built

Demandbase runs on a three-layer hierarchy. Understanding where each layer lives tells you where to build assets and why advertising always requires a Global step.

Layer 1 · Tenant
Global / All Regions
Journey Stage frameworks, Buying Group personas, and the Advertising DSP all live at the tenant level. Changes here affect every region. Advertising campaigns are built here, not inside individual workspaces.
Layer 2 · Workspace
US · EMEA · APAC
Each region operates in its own workspace with isolated account lists and reporting data. The US Workspace contains 5,241 tiered accounts. Workspace data does not sync automatically to Global or other regions.
Layer 3 · Assets
Lists · Keywords · Campaigns
Account lists, keyword sets, and campaigns live inside their workspace. For advertising to work, assets must also exist in Global with a [US] prefix. Dual-maintenance is required — there is no automatic sync.
Always confirm you're in US Workspace before pulling data. The Global workspace mixes in EMEA and APAC buying groups, produces incorrect account counts, and reflects inaccurate journey stage data for the US motion. The workspace dropdown is in the top-right corner of Demandbase.
02 · Account Tiers

Account universe & tiering

Every US account is tiered by revenue. Tier drives ABM motion — 1:1 for the largest, 1:Many for scale. Tier also determines which orchestration plays fire and whether BD or SDR handles the routing.

Total US universe: 5,241 tiered accounts in the US Workspace. Data sourced from D&B Hoovers (primary), ZoomInfo (fallback), and PitchBook for private-company coverage. All tier assignments are validated and maintained by the Lighthouse team.

When Marketing Qualifies an Account
MQA fires when an account crosses three combined thresholds: intent surge on target keyword sets, active engagement (ad clicks, site visits, content consumption), and buying group activity showing multiple personas in motion. When orchestration plays are live, a BD notification fires in SFDC automatically within 48 hours.
Customer Stage = Expansion Signal

Journey stage changes are the most powerful orchestration triggers. Don't just watch the current stage — watch the movement. An account that moved from Aware to Engaged in the last 7 days is more urgent than an account that has been sitting at Engaged for 60 days. Velocity is the signal.
04 · SDR / BD Routing

Pipeline Predict: who gets the account

Every US account is scored by two Pipeline Predict models — CX Motion and Vertical/Transformation. The scores tell you which team acts and with what urgency. The logic is simple: 40+ goes to SDR, 60+ goes directly to BD.

40+
CX or Vertical Pipeline Predict Score
SDR Outreach
Account is showing enough buying signal to warrant a warm outreach sequence — but not enough for a BD-led pursuit. SDR initiates contact, builds context, and surfaces the right personas before the account escalates.
Journey Stage = Engaged
1-2 keyword sets active
T2 or T3 account tier
Moderate buying group activity
SDR priority: warm outreach using the specific keyword themes driving engagement — not a cold sequence. Intent data tells you what they're researching; use it.
60+
CX or Vertical Pipeline Predict Score
BD-Led Pursuit
Account has crossed the threshold where BD energy is justified. Multiple intent signals, high persona completeness, and strong Pipeline Predict score together indicate an active buying motion — not passive research.
Journey Stage = MQA
3+ keyword sets active
T1-A or T1 account tier
High buying group completeness
BD priority: BD receives the account with full intent context already populated — keyword themes, engaged personas, journey stage. The warm-up work is done.
Two models, one account. The same account can score differently on CX Motion vs. Vertical/Transformation. If Honeywell scores 42 on CX and 71 on Vertical, that tells you the deal entry point is transformation — not CX outsourcing. Lead with the higher-scoring motion. Don't default to CX just because it's the flagship pitch.
Intent Surge > Raw Score
Velocity matters more than the number
A T2 account that jumped 20 Pipeline Predict points in 7 days is more actionable than a T1-A sitting at a steady 65. Watch the delta. Surge behavior means the buying window is open right now.
Missing Personas = Block
Incomplete buying group caps the score
If persona completeness is below 100% on a T1-A or T1 account, the Pipeline Predict score is artificially capped. Enrich the missing role through Lighthouse first — then let the model re-score. A 58 can become an 82 after one persona is added.
High Intent + Missing Personas
Enrich before you route
High intent with incomplete buying group coverage means the account is active but you don't have the right contact. Don't route it to BD with a half-built account. Enrich first. Route second. The window is usually open long enough.
05 · List Creation

Always start in Global. Then push to US Workspace.

This is the single most common mistake. If you build a list in US Workspace first and try to use it for advertising, it won't connect. The advertising DSP reads from Global. Build there first, every time.

1
Build in Global
Create the account list or keyword set in the Global workspace. Name it with the [US] prefix.
2
Name it right
Format: [US] Tier 1A — Healthcare. This naming convention keeps US assets identifiable in the Global view.
3
Mirror in US Workspace
Manually duplicate the same list in US Workspace without the [US] prefix. This is your operational copy for reporting and orchestration.
4
Connect advertising
Ad campaigns reference the Global [US] version. Orchestration and reporting reference the US Workspace version.
No sync exists between US Workspace and Global. Every update to a keyword set or account list requires a manual update in both places. When you add a new account to Tier 1A — Healthcare in US Workspace, you must also add it to [US] Tier 1A — Healthcare in Global for it to be included in active ad campaigns. Dual-maintenance is permanent.
Account Lists
Naming convention
US Workspace: 2026 Tier 1A — Healthcare

Global: [US] 2026 Tier 1A — Healthcare

Include the year. Tier. Vertical or motion type. Keep it readable — if someone in EMEA sees it in Global, the [US] prefix tells them it's not theirs to touch.
Keyword Sets
Naming convention
US Workspace: AI Customer Service — CX Core

Global: [US] AI Customer Service — CX Core

Keyword sets follow the same dual-maintenance rule. The 42+ sets currently in US Workspace each need a mirrored [US] version in Global to be usable for ad targeting.
Person Lists for advertising: Build these natively in Demandbase under the People tab. Filter by T1A–T3 account lists + persona/title criteria. Adding Known Contact person lists to ad campaigns moves targeting from anonymous account-level to named-person level — a significant upgrade in precision.
06 · Buying Groups

Who you're targeting inside the account

Buying groups define the personas TP pursues within each account. Demandbase targets at the intersection of account list and persona — not account-level only. If your campaign isn't reaching the right people, check persona filters and completeness first.

Buying Group 1
CX Core
Primary decision-makers for customer experience transformation. CCO, CXO, VP Customer Experience, VP Contact Center Operations, Director of Customer Service. These are the economic buyers for the core CX outsourcing motion.
Buying Group 2
LOBs — Lines of Business
Functional leaders controlling budget for back-office and operational outsourcing. CFO, CIO, CHRO, VP Finance, VP IT, VP Operations. This group controls the budget for transformation programs — they're often the unseen power in a deal.
Buying Group 3
Vertical Specialists
Industry-specific personas for regulated verticals. Chief Medical Officer (Healthcare), Chief Compliance Officer (Finance), Chief Digital Officer (Retail). When a deal is vertical-led, these personas gate the decision more than the CX buyer.
Persona completeness directly impacts MQA eligibility. Under 100% completeness on a T1-A or T1 account means at least one required persona has no matched contact in SFDC. The buying group score is capped, and MQA progression is blocked until the missing role is enriched. Send incomplete accounts to Lighthouse with the account name and missing job function.
Powerline Framework
Not all personas are equal
Demandbase's Powerline weighting assigns higher influence to Economic Buyers (CCO, CFO) and Technical Buyers (CIO, VP Ops). Engagement from these personas moves Pipeline Predict scores faster than engagement from lower-influence roles. If the CFO is clicking your ads, that's a different conversation than a Director of Operations downloading a white paper.
Global View Warning
EMEA and APAC groups appear in Global
Buying group tabs for EMEA and APAC are visible in the Global workspace because personas are configured at the tenant level. This is expected behavior — not a data error. Always filter to your US account list first when pulling buying group data in Global. Otherwise the counts and engagement numbers will be wrong.
07 · Intent & Keywords

42+ keyword sets. Buyer language only.

Intent monitoring is where Demandbase earns its value. TP tracks 42+ keyword sets across all buying group themes. The most common mistake is building keyword sets in seller language instead of buyer research language — seller keywords return zero intent data because buyers don't search that way.

✓ Buyer Research Language — Use These

  • AI customer service platform
  • Contact center cost reduction
  • CX transformation roadmap
  • Omnichannel customer support
  • Back-office automation ROI
  • Workforce management software
  • Digital transformation outsourcing
  • Healthcare revenue cycle management

✗ Seller / Procurement Language — Avoid

  • Outsourcing RFP
  • Vendor consolidation
  • BPO contract
  • Service provider comparison
  • Teleperformance alternative
  • Contact center outsourcing vendor
  • BPO partner selection
  • Managed services procurement
Intent Surge vs. Raw Score
Watch the velocity, not the number
A Tier 2 account that spiked 20 intent points in 7 days is more actionable than a Tier 1-A sitting at a steady high score. The surge means they are actively researching right now — the buying window is open. High static scores without recent movement can represent a company that always shows some intent but isn't in an active buying cycle.
Cross-Set Overlap
Intentional, not a data error
Keywords are designed to overlap across sets. "AI customer service" fires both the CX Core set and the TaaS set simultaneously. This mirrors real buyer behavior — someone researching AI contact center capabilities is relevant to both motions. When an account lights up multiple sets at once, that's a strong multi-motion signal worth escalating.
08 · Rules

The non-negotiables

These rules protect the US Workspace's data integrity and keep advertising working correctly. They're not suggestions.

1
US Workspace stays isolated. Do not allow other regional teams to make configuration changes inside the US Workspace. All cross-region coordination happens at the Global/tenant level only. Any tenant-level change — journey stages, buying group personas, global settings — requires ABM leadership sign-off before execution.
2
Every US asset in Global uses the [US] prefix. Account lists, keyword sets, person lists, and saved filters. Examples: [US] Tier 1A — Healthcare, [US] AI Customer Service — CX Core. This keeps US assets identifiable and prevents other regions from modifying them accidentally.
3
No automatic sync between US Workspace and Global. Every update in US Workspace requires a manual update to the corresponding [US] asset in Global. This applies to keyword sets, account lists, and person lists. Dual-maintenance is permanent architecture — not a temporary workaround.
4
Advertising campaigns build at tenant/Global level. The DSP is not workspace-scoped. If you try to build a campaign referencing a US Workspace-only asset, it won't connect to the ad layer. Always reference the [US] Global version in campaign targeting.
5
Geo-targeting: United States only. When configuring advertising campaigns, North America defaults to Canada + Mexico + United States. Always deselect Mexico — Mexico is LATAM, not US. Leaving it selected pulls LATAM-owned accounts into US targeting and pollutes data. Set to United States only at the country level, every campaign, every time.
6
SFDC campaign member status = "Selected for Campaign." Orchestration-written campaign members always use this status. Never use "Responded," "Filled Out Form," or any status that implies an action the contact hasn't taken. Filter logic on each automation excludes existing members from re-triggering.
7
All DB admin and integration requests route through the DB Ops Manager. Analysts and campaign operators do not have admin access. If a configuration change, integration setup, or Salesforce connector issue is needed, route to the DB Ops Manager — not directly to other admin contacts.
