Powerline Framework

Two sides of the line. One determines the deal.

Above Powerline — C-Suite
CEO · CFO · COO · CXO/CCO · CRO · CIO · CISO · CTO · CDO/CAIO · CPO
Final decision authority and budget sign-off. These personas either approve or kill a deal. You need at least one above the line to close.
Below Powerline — Execution Layer
EVP · SVP · VP Sales · VP Customer Experience · VP Operations · VP IT · Director Revenue Ops · Director Sales Ops · Director Inside Sales
Champions, initiators, evaluators. They start the deal and build the business case — but need C-Suite approval to close.
Procurement & Sourcing — Universal Approval Layer Across All Buying Groups

Regardless of which buying group leads the deal — CX Core, LOBs, or Vertical Specialists — Procurement & Sourcing is always present as the Budget Holder and contract approval layer. This is not optional threading. They control the vendor selection process and contract execution. Thread them early or get surprised late.

Buying Group Selection

Match the buying group to the motion.

Which groups you activate depends entirely on what deal you're running. Don't default to CX Core for every play.

CX Motion
CX Core + Vertical Specialists
CXO/CCO leads the decision. Vertical Specialists add the industry-specific buyer that the CCO alone doesn't cover — a Healthcare CX deal needs a Chief Patient Experience Officer in the room.
LOB / Transformation Motion
LOBs + Vertical Specialists
CFO, CIO, CHRO hold the functional budget. Vertical Specialists contextualize the deal for the industry — a back-office play at a bank needs the CFO and Chief Compliance Officer, not the CX team.
Vertical Awareness Only
Vertical Specialists only
Top-of-funnel awareness play. No deal in motion yet. You're warming industry-specific buyers before a signal fires. No LOBs, no CX Core — this runs before a buying committee assembles.
Full T1-A Pursuit
All three buying groups active
BD is engaged, deal is live. Thread the full committee simultaneously — CX Core, LOBs, and Vertical Specialists all in motion at once. Procurement is always present across all three.
