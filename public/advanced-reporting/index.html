US Filter Requirement: ALWAYS filter to Country = United States before reading numbers. Default view shows global data, which will inflate your metrics if other regions are running ads.

Why This Matters for US Team

If you see spend in countries other than the United States, it means:

  • Account list includes non-US HQ companies (check list composition)
  • Cross-border targeting is enabled (review campaign settings)
  • APAC/EMEA campaigns are bleeding into your view (apply ad plan filter)

3. Creative Performance

Ad group and creative-level analysis to identify top performers and optimization opportunities.

Key Metrics

Ad Group KPIs

Metric Definition Good Benchmark
Impressions Number of times ad was displayed 10K+ per week per ad group
CTR (Click-Through Rate) Clicks ÷ Impressions 0.05-0.15% (display ads)
Accounts Reached Unique target accounts that saw ad Varies by list size
Frequency Avg impressions per account 5-12 (avoid over-saturation)
Spend Total ad spend for this ad group Track to budget allocation

Top 5 by Impressions vs CTR

Side-by-side comparison showing:

  • Left Chart: Top 5 ad groups by impression volume
  • Right Chart: Top 5 ad groups by CTR

Key Insight: High impressions with low CTR = creative fatigue or poor relevance. High CTR with low impressions = winner that needs more budget.

Trend Line

Performance over time for selected ad group. Shows impressions, clicks, and CTR trends across date range.

Frequency Cap Guidance

Monitor Frequency metric closely:

  • Under 5: Low reach — increase budget or expand targeting
  • 5-12: Optimal — accounts are seeing ads enough to build awareness without fatigue
  • Over 15: Over-saturation — reduce frequency cap or pause ad group temporarily
Pro Tip: Sort by CTR descending to find your best-performing creatives, then use those headlines/body copy patterns for new ad groups.

5. Pacing & Account Performance

Budget pacing analysis and account-level engagement tracking. Ensure campaigns stay on track to hit goals.

Pacing Definitions

On Track

Campaign is spending at the expected rate to fully utilize budget by end date.

  • Calculation: (Actual Spend / Days Elapsed) ≈ (Total Budget / Total Days)
  • Action: No changes needed — monitor weekly

Under-Pacing

Campaign is spending slower than needed to fully utilize budget.

  • Causes: Narrow targeting, low bids, limited inventory
  • Action: Increase bid, expand targeting, or add ad groups

Predicted to Hit Goal

Based on current spend velocity, campaign will reach or exceed target accounts/impressions.

  • Action: Continue current strategy

6. Filters

Standard filter bar appears at top of all dashboards. Master these for accurate reporting.

Filter Bar Usage

  • Ad Plan: ALWAYS set to TP USA – Q-81341

7. Pro Tips & Best Practices

Weekly Workflow

Monday — Pacing Check:

  • Open Pacing & Account Performance dashboard
  • Filter to TP USA Q-81341 + Last 7 days
  • Identify under-pacing campaigns → increase bids or expand targeting

Wednesday — Creative Optimization:

  • Open Creative Performance dashboard
  • Sort by CTR descending
  • Pause bottom 20% performers, boost budget to top 20%

Friday — Journey Tracking:

  • Open JourneyIQ Reporting dashboard
  • Review Aware → Engaged → MQA funnel progression
  • Export accounts at MQA stage for BDR team handoff

Pro Tips

  1. Bookmark Your Filters: Save common filter combinations to avoid re-configuring every time
  2. Compare Week-Over-Week: Use date range comparison to spot trends early
  3. Focus on Velocity, Not Just Volume: A Tier 2 account spiking 20 points is hotter than a Tier 1 with steady intent
  4. Export to CSV: Use export button (top-right) to pull data into Excel for deeper analysis
  5. Cross-Reference with SFDC: Match MQA/Opportunity accounts in Demandbase with SFDC to validate attribution

8. FAQ

1. How often does data refresh?

Answer: Data refreshes every 24 hours (overnight). Expect ~12-24 hour delay between ad delivery and dashboard visibility. Check "Last Updated" timestamp at top of each dashboard.

2. Why is click data showing zero?

Answer: Demandbase One Tag (GTM-5J4WZ38R) is not yet deployed on tp.com/en-us. Until this tag goes live, click-through attribution will not work. Use impressions, account engagement, and journey stage movement as proxy metrics.

3. Can I export data?

Answer: Yes. Click the export button (top-right of each dashboard) to download CSV. Data exports include all filtered rows with full metric columns.

4. What does "All Other" mean in Country breakdown?

Answer: Accounts with no identified country OR accounts outside top 20 countries by spend. Drill down to see full list.

5. Why do I see APAC/EMEA data in my Global view?

Answer: Advertising operates at tenant/Global level. ALL regions' campaigns appear in Global view. ALWAYS filter to Ad Plan = TP USA Q-81341 to isolate US data.

6. What's the difference between "Predicted to Hit Goal" and "On Track"?

Answer:

  • On Track: Current spend rate matches expected budget burn rate
  • Predicted to Hit Goal: Based on engagement velocity, campaign will reach target account/impression goals

You can be On Track for budget but Under-Pacing for goals (or vice versa).

7. Why is a campaign showing spend outside the United States?

Answer: Three possible causes:

  • Account list includes non-US HQ companies (check list composition)
  • Cross-border targeting enabled in campaign settings (review settings)
  • IP-based targeting showing employees in other countries (expected for global enterprises)

8. Can I get automated alerts for under-pacing campaigns?

Answer: Not natively in Beta. Workaround: Set a recurring Monday calendar reminder to check Pacing & Account Performance dashboard manually.
